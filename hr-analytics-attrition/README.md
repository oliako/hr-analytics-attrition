## HR Analytics: Employee Attrition Analysis

This project analyzes employee attrition using SQL to identify key factors influencing employee turnover.

The goal is to understand where attrition is highest and what factors may contribute to employees leaving the company.

## Dataset

The dataset used in this project is the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset.

It includes information about employees such as:

* Demographics (age, gender)
* Job-related attributes (department, role, income)
* Work conditions (overtime, job satisfaction)
* Attrition status (whether the employee left the company)


## Tools

* SQL (SQLite)
* VS Code
* GitHub

## Analysis

### Overall Attrition

The overall employee attrition rate is **16.12%**, meaning approximately **1 in 6 employees leave the company**.

---

### Attrition by Department

The Sales department has the highest attrition rate (**20.63%**), compared to Research & Development (**13.84%**).

This suggests that employees in Sales may experience higher pressure or less stability.

---

### Attrition and Overtime

Employees who work overtime have a significantly higher attrition rate (**30.53%**) compared to those who do not (**10.44%**).

This indicates that workload may be a major factor influencing employee turnover.

---

### Attrition and Job Satisfaction

Employees with lower job satisfaction are significantly more likely to leave the company.

The attrition rate for employees with the lowest satisfaction (**22.84%**) is approximately twice as high as for those with the highest satisfaction (**11.33%**), indicating that job satisfaction is a key driver of employee retention.

## Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

* Investigate workload and overtime practices, especially in high-attrition groups
* Improve employee satisfaction through engagement and feedback initiatives
* Focus retention efforts on departments with higher attrition, such as Sales
* Review compensation and career development opportunities
